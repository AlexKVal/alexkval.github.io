Technical Co-founder Profile

I’m looking for a business partner who knows sales, marketing, and go-to-market. I bring two decades of hands-on engineering, team building, and running my own technical operation.

What I bring

I’ve been writing software professionally since 2000. My range goes from Atmel microcontroller firmware and GPS tracking hardware to enterprise SaaS serving Walmart and Amazon. As a hobby I built an 8-bit computer on breadboards from basic logic ICs: no pre-built CPU, just transistor-level understanding of how computers actually work. Right now I’m a Lead Software Engineer at Coupa Software, working in a 6.5 million line Rails codebase, shipping features for Fortune 500 companies. Before that I was one of three principal-level engineers at HalalBooking, a travel platform where I worked across the entire stack: booking, checkout, search, pricing, payments, integrations.

I’ve built and led technical teams. At a company in Kyiv I grew an IT department from zero to 10 engineers, hiring every person myself: sourcing, technical interviews, offers, onboarding. Managed the budget directly, negotiated vendor contracts, and made the hard calls when hires didn’t work out.

I’ve run a technical business for 14 years: an independent IT services operation for a restaurant chain with 17 locations. Client negotiations, team management, on-call, automation. The client stayed the entire time because my service was better and cheaper than the software vendor’s own support.

I’m a core team member of react-bootstrap (22,600 GitHub stars). Push access, npm publish rights. The project founder publicly called me “the bulldog that just keeps fixing everything he can as fast as he can.” 6.7 million monthly npm downloads. I know what it means to maintain software that many people depend on.

I use AI agents daily: Cursor IDE with custom skills, rules, and agent workflows. I ship production code with AI assistance every day. But what matters more for a startup: I use AI agent teams for parallel work. Agents build while I sleep, I unlock tasks from my phone. I’ve been doing this kind of delegated remote management for 20 years, first with human teams, now with AI. It’s second nature, not an experiment.

Tech stack

Strong: Ruby on Rails, JavaScript/TypeScript, React, PostgreSQL, MySQL, Python, Git, Linux

Capable: AWS, Node.js, Docker, Kubernetes, Elasticsearch, Redis, ClojureScript, C#/.NET, Swift, Kotlin, electronics (analogue + digital, hobby-level depth)

I’ve shipped production code in Ruby, JavaScript, TypeScript, Python, ClojureScript, and C#. I pick up new languages fast: learned ClojureScript on the job at HalalBooking, shipping production features from day one. Same approach for Swift and Kotlin: I understand mobile UX deeply (studied it formally, tested mobile at HalalBooking), and with AI assistance I can deliver production quality mobile apps.

I’m a polyglot by nature. Six programming languages in production, six human languages to various degrees. Learning new tools is what I do.

Infrastructure

I prefer renting hardware and using Docker/Kubernetes over AWS/GCP for startup budgets. Cloud providers make sense at scale, but early on they eat runway for things you can do cheaper with a VPS and good automation.

I have deep understanding of how Docker and Kubernetes work, both at the infrastructure level and practically. I haven’t done a production deployment from scratch yet, but with my background and current AI tooling, that’s days of work, not months.

Generalist engineers with good AI assistance can match cloud specialists for most startup-scale deployments. The era of needing a dedicated AWS expert to run your infra is ending.

What I’m looking for in a partner

Someone who complements what I lack:

Sales and go-to-market. I’ve never sold software to many customers. I retained a single client for 14 years, so I understand delivering value. But finding and converting customers at scale is not something I’ve done.

Marketing. Zero experience.

Product-market fit instincts. I can build whatever needs building. I need a partner who knows what’s worth building.

What I’m honest about

I haven’t taken a SaaS product from zero to paying customers. My builds have been internal tools, contributions to existing products (HalalBooking, Coupa), and open source libraries. I’m filling this gap now: building a job-radar app, an AI career assistant that uses a rich profile (beyond what LinkedIn or a CV can capture) to help with job search, interview prep, cover letters, and career advice. The need is real: I use it myself and my wife uses it too. The plan is to ship it for free first, to get the zero-to-users experience.

My most recent full-time work is in a massive enterprise monolith, not lean startup mode. But my HalalBooking years (small team, ship fast, own everything) and my 14-year Fishka operation are closer to startup reality.

Availability

I have 2-3 years of financial runway. Ready to leave my current job and go full-time if the project and partner are right.

Even before that: evenings and weekends go much further now with AI agents. I ran a parallel business alongside a full-time job for 3 years (2017-2020), managing everything remotely. Adding a second workstream is something I’ve done before.

Why me

Most developers who want to co-found a startup have ambition but no track record of running things. I’ve done the work:

Built a 10-person department from zero, hiring every engineer and managing the budget myself. Made bad hires and fired people, no HR to hide behind. Negotiated contracts on both sides: as a buyer (hardware, software, networking vendors) and as a seller (14 years of IT services). Was offered the VP Engineering role at HalalBooking by the CTO, who knew my management background and thought I could do his job.

And something that’s harder to teach: I kept a single client happy for 14 years. I maintain open source software downloaded 6.7 million times a month. My GitHub contribution graph was green every single day for 6 years straight (2015-2021), holidays included. I finish what I start.
